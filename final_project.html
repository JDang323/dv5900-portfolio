Not Just A Cartoon: Anime Trends

Introduction

Welcome to "Not Just A Cartoon: Anime Trends", an interactive data Visualization project that dives into the beautiful world of anime. Anime has been a growing industry with a wide variety of shows, genres, studios, stories, characters and much more. Through a series of insightful, engaging, and clear data visualizations, this project explores various aspects of the anime industry, including genre popularity and trends over time, release patterns, audience ratings and the intricate web of anime productions. This project aims at both ends of the spectrum when it comes to its target audience. Whether you're a devoted anime fan or a newcomer, I aim to provide a deeper understanding of how anime has evolved and what factors contribute to the growing appeal. Join me on this data-driven journey to uncover the trends and stories behind your favorite anime series.

Today, we'll walk through different interactive visuals and dashboards that will help you understand the realm and trends of the anime world. First, I will introduce an anime dashboard as the face of the project that gives the user a basic dashboard that will help give stats on specific animes. Afterwards, we will dive into looking at genre trends and how they've grown over the years. Next, we'll look at seasonal releases of anime in hopes that it will help us understand why anime are released at certain times of the year. An important aspect when trying to understand the anime industry is to see what the audience and fans like. We'll look at some visuals to identify trends. Along with this, we can look at how well an anime scores (how it is rated) vs. how many people set it as their favorite. Closing out the analysis would be looking at the Production Analysis of anime. This involves looking at what Studio, Licensor, and Producer create the best anime and what the fans really love. With the help of data visualization, we can try take a step into the anime realm. All of the visualizations are interactive in some way. Power BI allows for the creation of very dynamic and interactive Dashboards and visualizations.

Note that the data is not up to date. It was last updated 7/31/2023. The dataset used for all the visuals is the Anime Dataset 2023.

Seasonal Release Analysis

Often times, much like any other movie, animes will have a seasonal release date such as "Fall 2020" or "Summer 2021". It can get one person thinking - When do most animes get released? This interactive Power BI graph will be able to tell us. When you change the range of the years or even choose a single year, you will find that Winter dominates the release seasons! There are a few reasons for this:

  1. Holiday Season: Winter involves a lot holidays and break. This free time allows the audience to watch more shows, which is what studios look out for and release more anime around this time.
  2. Audience Preferences: People typically want to stay indoors during the colder months. Being indoors gives more opportunity to watch shows. There is a trend in TV shows watched and seasons.
  3. Production Timelines: Anime can take a while to plan and make. These plans are usually made earlier in the years, which naturally causese them to line up with a Winter Release.
