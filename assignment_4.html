Assignment 4

This assignment is the Liar Liar. We are tasked with choosing a topic that typically has disagreements. Using this topic, we have to find a data set that represents this and to create 2 valid figures, each showing opposite conclusions. We have to intentionally mislead the viewers to believe in a different conclusions by manipulating the data.

For my assignment, I used the Suicide Rates Overview 1985 to 2016 dataset. Using this dataset, I will create 2 different visualizations that portray two opposing conclusions.

Figure 1 - Average Suicides From 1985 - 2016 by Country and Sex

Original Figure

This is the original data that is unfiltered and automatically scaled to include all data appropriately. This graph is straight forward and shows the suicide rates for males vs. females by country. We can see that based on the chart, the suicide rate is relatively the same across the board, except for a few outliers such as the Russian Federation. The scale for the average number of suicides was left alone to ensure that the chart capture all of the data. For both the original graph and the misleading one, the color scheme was changed to match typical representations of male and female, blue and pink.

Figure 2 - Average Suicides From 1985 - 2016 by Country and Sex

Misleading Figure

This is the intentionally misleading figure. This line chart shows the average number of suicides for each country between 1985 and 2016. This chart is suggesting that the suicide rates for males is significantly much higher than female suicides (showing ~2x for each country). The original chart was showing that although the male suicide rates are generally still higher than the female suicide rates, it is not as significant as this chart shows, outside a few outliers such as the Russian Federation with dramatically higher male suicide rates. I removed outliers from the original list of countries to try and even out the data more so that I can adjust the scale to make it look like there is a dramatic difference in the averge number of suicides. Given that some countries have been eliminated to even the data out more, the two charts don't have the same countries being represented, which can be hard to catch because there are so many and Tableau does not always fill in ALL country names so it would be hard to cross check at first glance without the help of a tooltip.

Final Thoughts

After completing this assignment, it is clear to see how easily data visualizations can be manipulated to support different conclusions. By selectively presenting data and emphasizing different aspects over others, it's possible to lead viewers to different conclusions. It is fairly easy to mislead people. Visualizations are used to portray trends and raw data in an easy to read manner, for the general public to be able to quickly understand what is going on without having to look at the raw data. With this in mind, people are typically going to be looking at the charts and graphs quickly and can easily skim over differnt characteristics of a visualization. Because of that, authors can easily manipulate data for share a different narrative and blow topics out of perspctive. They can change metrics such as time, the values, the scales, colors, and sizing. These characteristics can sell different stories and conclusions that are represented by data fairly easily.
